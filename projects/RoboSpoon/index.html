RoboSpoon: Robotic Spoon for Food Acquisition Towards Self-Sufficiency

University of Maryland, College Park

Abstract

Robotic Assisted Feeding (RAF) has emerged as a transformative field in the realm of assistive robotics, addressing the fundamental need for individuals with mobility impairments to regain autonomy in feeding themselves. Recent work has focused on acquiring solid food items. But manipulation of semi-solid and deformable food items has not been well-studied due to inadequate modeling and remains a significant challenge. In this study, we introduce RoboSpoon, an innovative learning-from-demonstration (LfD) system designed to enhance the manipulation strategies for different categories of semi-solid and deformable foods. This system aims to fill the existing research gap by focusing on detailed manipulation strategies for foods such as yogurt, jelly, cereals, and tofu. We initiated the first steps towards addressing this gap by utilizing a spoon as a tool to manipulate these food types. The system leverages an LfD approach to adeptly scoop semi-solid foods, applying different strategies to prevent spillage and breakage. Our initial trials have exhibited promising results, with the system attaining an approximate 100% success rate in the scooping of semi-solid foods without spillage. Initially, the handling of deformable foods presented a challenge, often resulting in breakage or failure to scoop effectively. We successfully addressed this issue by implementing a wall-guided scooping strategy, which has proven adept at securing the food without causing breakage, enhancing the efficacy and scope of the RoboSpoon system.
